All 9 sacred Navagraha temples near Kumbakonam covered in one day trip

Suryanar Kovil

📍 Aduthurai  ·  Surya (Sun)

Dedicated to the Sun God (Surya). One of the most important Navagraha temples with unique architecture.

Kailasanathar Temple

📍 Thingalur  ·  Chandra (Moon)

Temple of the Moon God. Legend says Chandra worshipped Lord Shiva here to be cured of a curse.

📖 View on Wikipedia →
🔴

Vaitheeswaran Kovil

📍 Vaitheeswaran Koil  ·  Angarakan (Mars)

Famous for healing powers. Lord Shiva is worshipped as the healer God (Vaidhyanatha). Also known for Nadi astrology.

📖 View on Wikipedia →
💫

Swetharanyeswarar Temple

📍 Thiruvenkadu  ·  Budha (Mercury)

Dedicated to Mercury. The temple is set in a white forest (Swetha Vanam) and has ancient Chola inscriptions.

📖 View on Wikipedia →
🪐

Apathsahayesvarar Temple

📍 Alangudi  ·  Guru (Jupiter)

Temple of Jupiter (Guru Bhagavan). Visiting here is believed to bring wisdom, prosperity and good fortune.

📖 View on Wikipedia →
✨

Agneeswarar Temple

📍 Kanjanur  ·  Shukra (Venus)

Dedicated to Venus (Shukra). Agni (God of fire) is believed to have worshipped here, giving the temple its name.

📖 View on Wikipedia →
🪐

Dharbaranyeswarar Temple

📍 Thirunallar  ·  Shani (Saturn)

One of the most visited Navagraha temples. Famous for Shani Peyarchi. King Nala bathed here to overcome Saturn's curse.

📖 View on Wikipedia →
🌑

Naganathaswamy Temple

📍 Thirunageswaram  ·  Rahu

Temple of the shadow planet Rahu. Famous for Rahu Kala Puja performed every Sunday to reduce Rahu dosha effects.

Naganathaswamy Temple

Temple of shadow planet Ketu. Devotees visit to seek relief from Ketu dosha and for spiritual enlightenment.
